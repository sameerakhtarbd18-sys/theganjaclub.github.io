## How the system works (it is the same in Manchester as everywhere else)

The Misuse of Drugs Regulations 2001 were amended on 1 November 2018, moving cannabis-based products for medicinal use to Schedule 2. GMC-registered specialists — not GPs — can prescribe when clinically appropriate.

Manchester has no local legal differences from London or anywhere else in the UK. The same framework applies nationally. The advantage for Manchester patients is purely practical: video consultations mean you access specialists from anywhere, and delivery pharmacies ship nationwide.

## The consultation process

1. **Request your Summary Care Record** from your Manchester GP practice or NHS App
2. **Book a video consultation** — most clinics book within 1–2 weeks
3. **Attend the call** — 30–60 minutes with a GMC-registered specialist
4. **If prescribed:** medication arrives by courier, typically 1–5 days
5. **Follow-ups:** every 1–3 months initially, then less frequently

---

## Manchester-specific notes

**Pharmacy delivery:** Specialist pharmacies ship nationwide. Most Manchester addresses receive next-day delivery. No local collection points are currently available — everything comes by courier.

**Transport and driving:** The DVLA advises patients on cannabis-based medicines not to drive if impaired. Manchester's Metrolink and bus network makes this less of a practical constraint than in car-dependent areas.

**Employment:** Manchester has a large NHS and public sector workforce. NHS Trusts in Greater Manchester follow national NHS employment guidance on prescribed medicines. Private sector employers vary — your clinic can provide a treatment letter for HR.

**Cost of living:** Manchester's lower cost of living compared to London makes the ongoing medication costs slightly more manageable, but the clinic and pharmacy fees are national — they do not vary by location.
