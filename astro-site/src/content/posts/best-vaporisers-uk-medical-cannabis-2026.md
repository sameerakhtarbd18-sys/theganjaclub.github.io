## What makes a vaporiser "medical-grade"?

Before the list, a quick definition. "Medical-grade" is not a regulated term — any brand can claim it. What matters are three measurable things:

1. **Convection heating, not conduction.** Convection passes hot air through the herb. Conduction sits the herb on a hot surface. Convection is cooler, more even, and less likely to combust your medication. Every device on this list is convection or hybrid.
2. **Precise temperature control** — not preset levels, actual degree-by-degree adjustment. Different cannabinoids and terpenes vaporise at different temperatures. You want the ability to target them.
3. **An isolated vapour path.** Ceramic, glass, or medical-grade stainless steel. No plastic in the airway. No solder. No mystery alloys.

If a vaporiser does not meet all three, it is not on this list.

## What to avoid

- **Any vaporiser under £50.** At that price, you are getting a conduction pen with a mystery heating element. These devices combust flower, release questionable materials into the vapour path, and put patients off vaporising entirely.
- **Desktop vaporisers** (Volcano, etc.) — excellent devices, but this guide is for portables. If you only medicate at home, the Volcano Hybrid is the clinical gold standard. This guide assumes you need something portable.
- **Oil cartridge vapes.** UK medical cannabis flower prescriptions do not come in cartridges, and black-market THC vapes carry serious health risks (the EVALI crisis was caused by vitamin E acetate in illicit carts). Stick to dry herb.
