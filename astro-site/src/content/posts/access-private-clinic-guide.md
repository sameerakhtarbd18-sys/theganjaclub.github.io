<p>For patients who have exhausted conventional treatment options, accessing medical cannabis through a private clinic is increasingly straightforward. This guide covers the practical steps involved.</p><h2>Step one: Check eligibility</h2><p>Private clinics typically require that you have tried at least two conventional treatments for your condition without adequate response. Most clinics cover a wide range of conditions including chronic pain, anxiety, PTSD, insomnia, and neurological conditions. You'll need medical records to support your application.</p><h2>Step two: Choose a clinic</h2><p>The number of private clinics has grown significantly. When choosing a clinic, consider the clinic's specialisms, the qualifications of its clinicians, patient reviews, and cost. Reputable clinics will conduct thorough initial assessments and provide ongoing follow-up care.</p><h2>Step three: The consultation</h2><p>Your first consultation will involve a detailed review of your medical history, current treatments, and symptoms. The clinician will explain the potential benefits and risks of cannabis-based treatment, discuss different product options, and agree a treatment plan.</p><h2>Costs</h2><p>Initial consultations typically cost between £100 and £250. Follow-up appointments range from £50 to £150. Monthly medication costs vary widely depending on the product and dosage, typically ranging from £100 to £500 per month.</p><h2>Follow-up care</h2><p>Reputable clinics provide ongoing monitoring and support, with regular follow-up appointments to review progress and adjust treatment. This continuity of care is essential for safe and effective treatment.</p>
