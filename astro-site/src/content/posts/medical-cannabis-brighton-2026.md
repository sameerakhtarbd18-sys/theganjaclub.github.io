## Is medical cannabis actually legal in the UK?

Yes. The law changed on 1 November 2018, moving cannabis-based products for medicinal use from Schedule 1 (no recognised medical value) to Schedule 2 (recognised medical value) under the Misuse of Drugs Regulations 2001.

The practical effect: GMC-registered specialist doctors — not GPs — can prescribe cannabis-based medicinal products when they believe it is clinically appropriate. The law is the same in Brighton as it is in London, Manchester, or anywhere else in the UK. There is no local exception that makes access easier or harder in Brighton.

## How eligibility works

UK clinics assess patients against a consistent, evidence-based framework. You generally need:

1. **A diagnosed, ongoing condition** — chronic pain, anxiety, insomnia, PTSD, multiple sclerosis, epilepsy, fibromyalgia, migraine, IBD, chemotherapy-related nausea, and others are routinely assessed
2. **Evidence of prior treatments** — typically two or more standard licensed treatments that were tried without adequate relief or with intolerable side effects
3. **Access to your medical records** — your GP Summary Care Record documents your diagnosis and treatment history
4. **To be 18 or over** — adult clinics assess adults; paediatric access runs through NHS specialists

## What the consultation process looks like

Your initial consultation will almost certainly be a video call — you do not need to travel anywhere. Here is what to expect:

1. **Before the appointment:** Request your GP Summary Care Record (free — ask your Brighton practice reception or use the NHS App under Profile → GP health record). Upload it to the clinic's patient portal.
2. **The consultation:** A 30–60 minute video call with a GMC-registered specialist. They review your medical history, discuss your condition, and assess whether cannabis-based treatment is appropriate. Be honest — they are doctors, not gatekeepers.
3. **The outcome:** If prescribed, the specialist writes a prescription sent to a specialist pharmacy. Medication typically arrives by courier within 1–5 days. If not prescribed, they explain why and what would need to change.
4. **Follow-ups:** Usually every 1–3 months initially, then spreading to quarterly or six-monthly once your treatment is stable.

Brighton's bus network (Brighton & Hove Buses) and railway station on the Brighton Main Line provide public transport. The city is also very walkable. The DVLA advises against driving if impaired by cannabis-based medicines.
